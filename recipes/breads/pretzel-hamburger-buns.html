Pretzel Hamburger Buns

Pretzel Hamburger Buns

Once you try making bread you'll be hooked!

Yield: 8 • Prep: 2 hr • Cook: 20 min • Total: 2 hr 20 min

Ingredients

  • I ½ cups warm water or milk (110 degrees)
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons or ¼ ounce active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons granulated sugar
  • 4¼ cups all-purpose or bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt ( table or kosher)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • ¼ cup baking soda
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • salt for sprinkling (pretzel or kosher)

Instructions

  1. TURN ON THE OVEN LIGHT, NOT THE OVEN, JUST THE LIGHT. In the bowl of your stand-up mixer fitted with the dough hook, add the water (or milk), yeast, and sugar.
  2. Stir and let rest for 5-10 minutes, or until foamy.
  3. Add the flour, salt, and melted butter.
  4. Mix until well combined and forms a ball.
  5. Transfer to a medium bowl coated with non-stick cooking spray, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place, like the oven with the light on, for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  6. Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper and set aside.
  7. Pull the dough out of the bowl onto a lightly floured surface, and divide it into 8 equal portions (about 4.2 ounces each).
  8. Form each portion into a ball by first pinching the top to the bottom and then rolling it in the palm of your hand.
  9. Place the dough on a prepared cookie sheet, seam-side down, in a staggered position.
  10. Cover with a towel and allow to rest in a warm place, such as the oven with the light on, for 30 minutes or until they double in size. Place the buns on the cookie sheet in the refrigerator for twenty to thirty minutes.
  11. Preheat the oven to 425°F and place the oven rack in the middle position.
  12. In a large saucepan that fits 2 rolls, bring 2 quarts of water to a low boil.
  13. Remove from the heat and slowly add the baking soda.
  14. Return to the heat and continue with a steady boil.
  15. Place 2 of the rolls at a time in the poaching liquid, seam side down.
  16. Poach for 20 to 30 seconds, carefully turn the roll over during the bath.
  17. Remove with a slotted spoon to a cooling rack, and let the pretzels dry.
  18. Repeat with the remaining rolls.
  19. Using a pastry brush, brush each roll with the beaten egg, ensuring that all sides are completely coated, and then sprinkle each roll with salt.
  20. Using a sharp straight edged knife, cut a "X" shape in the top of each roll.
  21. Bake for 15 to 20 minutes.
