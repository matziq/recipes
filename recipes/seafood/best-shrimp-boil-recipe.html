BEST Shrimp Boil Recipe!

BEST Shrimp Boil Recipe!

You absolutely must try this delicious shrimp boil recipe asap! Made with juicy shrimp, sausage, corn, and potatoes cooked in the most delicious shrimp boil sauce ever, you’ll really love this easy shrimp boil recipe!

Yield: 6 • Prep: 15 min • Cook: 30 min • Total: 45 min

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs shrimp (the largest shrimp you can get)
  • 3 ears of corn
  • 1 pound smoked sausage
  • 1 ½ cups chicken stock (or reserved stock)
  • 1 pound potatoes
  • 1 – 2 Tablespoons Oil
  • 4 tablespoons Shrimp seasoning
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons of Old Bay Seasoning (optional)
  • ¾ tablespoon of hot sauce (optional)
  • 1 head of garlic minced
  • 1 large onion, minced
  • 1 ½ sticks of butter

Instructions

  1. Prepare all of your ingredients — That includes mincing the garlic (grab this garlic press to make it easier and thank me later), chopping up the onions, cleaning the shrimp, chopping the potatoes, chopping the sausages into 1-inch pieces, and making the shrimp seasoning.
  2. Par-cook the vegetables — Next, grab a deep pot and fill it ¾ of the way with water. Add 1 tablespoon of old bay seasoning (or homemade shrimp seasoning) to the water and cook the corn and potatoes for 10 — 12 minutes. Drain the potatoes and corn. You can reserve the 1 ½ cups of that broth if you do not have chicken stock on hand.
  3. Pan-sear the sausage — Use the same pot to pan sear the sausage with some oil for a minute or two on each side. Then set those aside for now.
  4. Make the shrimp boil sauce — Add the butter to the same pot over medium heat and let it melt (you can use either a deep skillet, stock pot oven dutch oven.) Then add the minced garlic (If you hate mincing garlic like I do, you’ll need a handy garlic press to make this  step easier), and chopped onions and cook until the onions are translucent and the garlic is fragrant.
  5. Then add 3 tablespoons of shrimp seasoning and 1 tablespoon of old bay to the skillet. Mix well and cook for another minute or two.
  6. Pour the chicken broth (or the reserved broth from earlier) into the skillet and mix. Bring this to a simmer.
  7. Cook the shrimp — Next, add the shrimp to the sauce and cook for 2 minutes.
  8. Add the sausage, potatoes, and corn — Immediately, add the par-cooked potatoes, corn, and cooked sausage to the skillet and coat with the sauce. Let this cook for 4 minutes so everything comes together nicely.
  9. Serve as desired. You can serve it with some crusty sourdough bread, or with these yummy ramen noodles.
