Best White Chocolate and Macadamia Cookies

Best White Chocolate and Macadamia Cookies

Ideally golden on the edges with a wonderfully soft and chewy center, these White Chocolate Macadamia Cookies are pure joy in every bite! Perfectly melt-in-your-mouth sweet and overflowing with creamy white chocolate and buttery, salty nuts, this is the kind of cookie recipe you'll dream about!

Yield: 30 cookies • Prep: 15 min • Cook: 12 min • Total: 27 min

Ingredients

  • 1 2/3 Cup Cake Flour (- spooned and leveled (SEE NOTES))
  • 1 1/3 Cup Bread Flour (- spooned and leveled (SEE NOTES))
  • 1 tsp Baking Powder
  • ¼ tsp Baking Soda
  • ¾ tsp Kosher Salt
  • 1 Cup Unsalted Butter (– COLD and cut into cubes (SEE NOTES))
  • ¾ Cup Light Brown Sugar (- packed)
  • 2/3 Cup Granulated Sugar
  • 2 Large Eggs (– cold and lightly beaten)
  • 1 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • 1 1/4 Cup White Chocolate Chips or Chunks (- plus more for topping cookie dough balls)
  • 1 Cup Toasted Macadamia Nuts (- roughly chopped (SEE NOTES))

Instructions

  1. Prepare cookie dough – dry ingredients: To a medium sized mixing bowl, add both flours, baking powder, baking soda, and kosher salt. Grab a whisk and stir the ingredients together. Then set the bowl aside.
  2. Prepare cookie dough – wet ingredients: Place the cold, cubed butter in a large mixing bowl using a hand-held mixer (or in the bowl of a stand mixer with the paddle attachment).  Beat the butter on medium-low speed for 1 to 2 minutes, or until the cubes come together to form one mass.  Add both sugars and beat at medium speed for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until the sugar is completely incorporated but small clumps of butter are present, stopping to scrape down the sides of the bowl as necessary.  With the mixer running, slowly add the eggs and vanilla to the bowl, stopping to scrape down the sides of the bowl once.  Beat until batter is mixed but still lumpy.
  3. Add dry ingredients to wet: Reduce speed to medium-low and slowly add the flour to the cookie dough.  Mix until ALMOST incorporated - taking care not to over-mix the cookie dough.
  4. Add in white chocolate chips and macadamia nuts: Dump the white chocolate chips and macadamia nuts into the bowl and use a large rubber spatula to fold the ingredients until the dough is JUST mixed together.
  5. Chill dough: Press a piece of plastic wrap against the top of the cooke dough to cover it and then place the dough in the refrigerator for at least 2-3 hours, or up to 3 days.  (Note: If chilling for longer than 2 hours, allow the cookie dough to sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes to make the cookies easier to roll out.)
  6. Preheat oven and prepare cookie pans: Arrange oven rack to the center position and then preheat the oven to 350-degrees Fahrenheit.  Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats.  Then set them aside.
  7. Scoop and roll dough into balls: Roll the dough into TALL balls, about 3 tablespoons each (or 1.5 ounces each), rolling the balls to be taller rather than wide or perfectly circular – this will ensure cookies will bake up thick versus thin.  Transfer the balls of cookie dough onto the prepared cookie sheet and arrange them to be 3 inches apart. Next, press a few white chocolate chips on top of the dough balls if desired.
  8. Bake macadamia white chocolate chip cookies: Transfer the cookie dough balls to the preheated oven and bake for 11-13 minutes, or until the edges are just beginning to turn light golden brown and the middles still look soft and underdone.  (Note: The cookies should look under-baked, but they will continue to bake on the cookie sheet in next step. DO NOT over-bake the cookies.)
  9. Cool white chocolate macadamia cookies: Remove the pan from oven and place onto a wire rack. Allow the cookies to cool on the cookie sheet for 5 minutes and then transfer them to the wire rack to cool completely.
  10. Enjoy white chocolate and macadamia nut cookies: Enjoy cookies with a tall glass of milk, hot chocolate, a cookie cocktail, or espresso martini. Alternatively, cookies will stay fresh in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week.
