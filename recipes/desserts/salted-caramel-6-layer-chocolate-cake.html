Salted-Caramel 6-Layer Chocolate Cake

Salted-Caramel 6-Layer Chocolate Cake

This salted caramel chocolate cake is the perfect celebration cake. It has six layers filled with salted caramel and is finished with rich chocolate frosting.

Yield: 1 9-inch cake • Prep: 45 min • Total: 200 min

Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter, room temperature, for pans
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for pans
  • 3 cups granulated sugar
  • 1.5 cups unsweetened Dutch-process cocoa powder
  • 1 tablepoon baking soda
  • 1.5 teaspoons baking powder
  • Coarse salt
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1.5 cups low-fat buttermilk
  • 0.5 cup plus 2 tablespoons safflower oil
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 4 cups granulated sugar
  • 0.25 cup light corn syrup
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • Coarse salt
  • 2 sticks cold unsalted butter, cut into tablespoons
  • 0.25 cup plus 2 tablespoons Dutch-process cocoa powder
  • 2 sticks unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 0.5 cup confectioners' sugar
  • Coarse salt
  • 1 pound semisweet chocolate, chopped, melted, and cooled
  • Garnish: flaked sea salt, such as Maldon

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Butter three 9-inch round cake pans, and dust with flour, tapping out excess.
  2. Sift flour, granulated sugar, cocoa, baking soda, baking powder, and 1 1/2 teaspoons coarse salt into the bowl of a mixer. Beat on low speed until just combined.
  3. Raise speed to medium, and add eggs, buttermilk, 1 1/2 cups warm water, oil, and vanilla. Beat until smooth, about 3 minutes.
  4. Divide batter among pans. Bake until cakes are set and a toothpick inserted into the center of each comes out clean, about 35 minutes.
  5. Let cool in pans set on a wire rack for 15 minutes. Turn out cakes onto racks, and let cool completely.
  6. Combine granulated sugar, corn syrup, and 1/4 cup water in a medium saucepan over high heat. Cook, without stirring, until mixture is dark amber, about 14 minutes.
  7. Remove from heat, and carefully pour in cream (mixture will spatter); stir until smooth. Return to heat, and cook until a candy thermometer reaches 238 degrees, about 2 minutes.
  8. Pour caramel into a medium bowl, stir in 1 teaspoon coarse salt, and let cool slightly, about 15 minutes. Stir in butter, 1 tablespoon at a time. Let cool completely.
  9. While caramel is cooling, whisk together cocoa and 1/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons warm water in a bowl until cocoa dissolves.
  10. Beat butter, confectioners' sugar, and a generous pinch of coarse salt in a clean bowl with a mixer on medium speed until pale and fluffy. Gradually beat in melted chocolate and then cocoa mixture until combined. Let stand for 30 minutes before using.
  11. Trim tops of cakes using a serrated knife to create a level surface. Cut each in half horizontally to form 2 layers.
  12. Transfer 1 layer to a serving platter, and spread 3/4 cup caramel over top. Top with another cake layer, and repeat with remaining caramel and cake layers, leaving top uncovered. Refrigerate until set, about 1 hour.
  13. Frost top and sides of cake in a swirling motion. Sprinkle with sea salt.
