First Fruit Purée

First Fruit Purée

**Makes 6 portions **Suitable for freezing

Fruit purées make ideal first meals. Choose only sweet fruit that is completely ripe for your baby. Once he is accustomed to eating an apple or pear purée on its own, try combining the two fruits to give him a taste of a mixed flavor. This purée will be a little thinner than vegetable purées.

2 medium sweet eating apples or ripe pears (peeled and cored)

Chop your chosen fruit into small pieces. Put in a heavy-bottomed saucepan with water or apple juice. Cover and cook over low heat until the fruit is tender. Or steam the fruit over water until tender.

Blend the fruit to a smooth purée with a hand blender or food processor. Add a little apple juice, boiled water or water from the bottom of the steamer to the purée if too thick.

Pour purée into an ice-cube tray and freeze.

VARIATION: Mash a raw banana or half a papaya and purée with a little breast milk or formula. If necessary, the banana can be heated in a microwave for a few seconds to make it easier to mash. These variations are NOT suitable for freezing.

TIP: All fruits for very first purées except for bananas, papayas, and avocados must be cooked. A month after beginning to wean your baby, you can use raw pears, peaches, mangoes, plums and melons, if they are ripe and juicy.
