Crab and Mango Empanadas

Crab y Mango Empanadas

Empanada Filling

½ cup chopped peeled mango

¼ cup frozen whole-kernel corn, thawed

¼ cup chopped green onions

2 tablespoons fat-free or lite mayonnaise

1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro

4 ounces lump crabmeat, drained

1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and minced

Empanada Dough

2 cups flour

6 tablespoons ice water

1 teaspoon cider vinegar

2 tablespoons powdered sugar

½ teaspoon salt

½ cup vegetable shortening

Lightly spoon flour into dry measuring cups, and level with a knife. Combine ½ cup flour, water, and vinegar, stirring with a whisk until well blended to form a slurry.

Combine 1 ½ cups flour, powdered sugar and salt in a bowl, stirring with a whisk; cut in shortening with a pastry blender until mixture resembles coarse meal. Add slurry; toss with a fork until flour mixture is moist.

Gently press mixture into a 4-inch circle on a heavy-duty plastic wrap, and cover with additional plastic wrap. Roll the dough, still covered, into and 18 x 12-inch rectangle; freeze 10 minutes or until plastic wrap can be easily removed. Remove 2 sheets of plastic wrap, and place dough on a lightly floured surface; let stand 1 minute. Cut the Empanada Dough into 12 circles using a 4-inch biscuit cutter or glass.

Combine all “filling” ingredients while empanada dough is freezing. Spoon 1 tablesppon filling onto half of each circle, leaving a ½-inch corder. Combine water and egg; brush egg mixture around edge of each circle. Fold dough over filling; press edges together with a fork or fingers to seal.

Place empanadas on a baking sheet coated with cooking spray; brush with remaining egg mixture. Bake at 425o for 25 minutes or until golden.

12 servings
